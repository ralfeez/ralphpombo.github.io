Seven distinct guest types define the co-living market in the United States today. 
They differ in income profile, lifestyle, motivation, risk tolerance, and what constitutes a great experience for them. 
A strategy that works brilliantly for travel nurses will fail with sober living residents. 
A marketing channel that fills beds for young professionals will produce zero results for workforce tenants. 
The differences are not cosmetic — they are structural, and they demand different operational approaches.
This paper covers each of the seven guest types in depth: who they are, why co-living works for them, what they need, 
how to market to them, and where to find them.
